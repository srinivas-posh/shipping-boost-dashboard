Section 4 of 5

Why last_self_shared_at is not the right signal

1 — Too concentrated, too little control

51.5% of all D7 order items come from listings at age_shared = 1. The remaining 48.5% is spread across days 2–200+. Sellers can self-share any listing at any time — every share resets the age to 1, which means an enormous share of inventory lands at age 1 simultaneously. A boost on this signal would amplify already-dominant listings and give sellers a trivial mechanism to game ranking daily. There is no natural throttle.

2 — Worse ranking outcomes

Shared variants cause the largest page 1 displacement of all: shared_sigmoid_x1 = −4.9% clicks vs updated_recip_x2 = −3.9%. Every shared variant underperforms every updated variant on all three metrics — clicks, FMs, and D7 order items — across every page bucket. The signal promotes the wrong listings.

Recommendation

Both selected variants use the same harmonic combination formula fs = 1 / max(1/signal + 1/d60, 1). The only difference is the shape of the update-time signal. Adjust the sigmoid parameters below to see how the curve moves.

15 days (inflection point)
0.50 steepness
sigmoid = 1 / (1 + exp(b × (age − a)))
recip = min(10000 / (ε·age·86400 + 0.112) / 86400, 1)
updated_sigmoid (dynamic) updated_recip (static)

Recip decays slowly and smoothly across all ages. Sigmoid decays sharply around the inflection point a. Both are capped at 1.0 and floor near 0 — identical boundaries, different slope shapes.

Proceed to A/B test: updated_recip_x2 and updated_sigmoid_x2

  • ✓updated_at is a real signal. OI/FM decays monotonically from 0.0110 (day 0) to ~0.0050 (day 200) — a genuine gradient the boost formula can exploit.
  • ✓Both x2 variants have the smallest offline displacement cost at −3.9% clicks on page 1, vs −4.9% for the worst shared variant. They are tied with each other across all three metrics.
  • ✓Sigmoid and recip are statistically indistinguishable offline. Ship both as separate A/B arms to get a live signal on formula shape — offline eval cannot differentiate them.
  • ⚠Drop all shared_at variants. No decay gradient, day-1 anomaly of 467M never-shared listings, and consistently worse ranking outcomes. Not worth an A/B slot.
  • ⚠Offline underestimates the true uplift. Boosted listings haven't been seen at top positions in logged data, so their engagement is understated. True gain is only visible in an online test.
